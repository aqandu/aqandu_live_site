The Science

We're taking a three-pronged approach to our work here at AQ & U;

1. Sensor Design and Performance

We are beginning by developing a network of low-cost, air-quality sensors based on commercially available components. These networked sensors will be deployed to complement the Utah Division of Air Quality's regulatory monitoring stations and community-based networks, like PurpleAir. Over the coming years, our sensors will evolve into to distributed, wearable air-quality sensors and systems. This strategy allows to begin collecting data immediately and to augment this data as our sensor technology improves. Our goal is to move to a fully passive, particulate matter sensor that costs a few dollars and communicates via RFID.

Because of the uncertain quality of measurements from low-cost sensors, we are using a three-pronged approach for sensor validation:

  • 1) a rigorous laboratory evaluation in a
  • 2) in situ field validation with gold-standard instrumentation, and
  • 3) rapid pre-deployment sensor validation.
